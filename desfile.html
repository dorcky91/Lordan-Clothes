The luxury Dutch brand proposes dresses inside out in its latest fashion show in Paris.

The brand's designers gave a literal twist to fashion by transforming classic princess dresses into anti-gravity dresses.

The innovative and refreshing collection combines haute couture and surrealistic art to showcase a new way of wearing and appreciating classic princess dresses.

It is true that fashion reaches as far as the limits of creativity. Especially when we enter the haute couture season and designers unleash the most extravagant and daring proposals that we can't even imagine until we see them on the runway. During Paris Haute Couture Fashion Week, the Dutch brand Viktor & Rolf gave a mental reset to fashion by presenting tulle dresses placed in the most unusual positions imaginable.

More than a fashion show, it was a complete theatrical production. A spectacle of tulle dresses that seemed to be taken from the most irreverent stages of Alice in Wonderland. The models walked the runway wearing floating dresses, crisscrossed, diagonally draped, layered over structured corsets, and even worn upside down over the lower half of the body.

Certainly, a surreal spectacle worthy of a fashion show of such magnitude like Haute Couture Fashion Week.
